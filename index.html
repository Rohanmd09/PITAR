The Problem

The Pit Speaks.
Tech Can't Listen.

Open-outcry trading is a centuries-old language — a dense vocabulary of hand gestures encoding price, quantity, direction, and strategy in split seconds. The infrastructure around it hasn't kept up. Signals get missed. Bookings lag. Compliance records are thin.

PitAR listens for you — via webcams already on the floor.

ERR_01Transcription Latency
Manual booking from pit signals introduces seconds of delay and frequent errors — generating bust trades that cost firms millions annually.
ERR_02Signal Ambiguity
In a dense pit, distinguishing your counterpart's gesture from background noise demands focus that degrades across a full session.
ERR_03No Audit Trail
Pit trades lack the timestamped records electronic markets provide — creating compliance exposure and DK dispute risk.

Why Now

Four Technologies.
One Convergence.

PitAR wasn't possible five years ago. Two hardware and two software breakthroughs have crossed simultaneously to make real-time pit execution viable today.

01
Edge AI on Commodity Hardware
Neural processing chips now run full computer vision pipelines at 120fps on standard server hardware — no specialized accelerators needed for webcam input at pit scale.
02
Modern Hand Pose Models
MediaPipe Hands and successor architectures deliver 21-keypoint skeletal models at frame-rate speeds — enabling sub-signal detection of compound two-handed gestures.
03
FIX Protocol Direct Access
Co-location and DMA via FIX means a parsed order hits the matching engine within microseconds of trader approval — faster than any manual entry path ever achieved.
04
Open-Outcry's Revival
Volatility-driven volume has revived pit trading at CME, CBOE, and regional exchanges after a decade of electronic dominance — creating the exact market PitAR was built for.
